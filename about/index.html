South Carolina Seismic Network

The SCSN was founded in 1974 by Pradeep Talwani at USC and collaborators at the US Geological Survey. The 1886 Charleston Earthquake demonstrated that substantial earthquake hazards exist in the region. The SCSN operates a small network of seismographs throughout the state to monitor continuing seismicity in the region. We partner with The Center for Earthquake Research and Information (CERI), the authoritative network for locating earthquakes in the southeastern US and with the USGS Advanced National Seismic System (ANSS). All data generated by the SCSN is available from Earthscope.

Dr. Talwani retired in 2009 and the SCSN was under the direction of Dr. Tom Owens until his retirement in 2022. Dr. Scott White was director from 2022-2024. Dr. Dan Frost is the current director. Dr. Philip Crotwell is the network manager.

More Information:

  • USGS Earthquake Hazards Program

    The USGS is responsible for monitoring earthquakes and related hazards within the US and globally. They maintain the official catalog of all earthquakes. The South Carolina Seismic Network is a regional network within the USGS Advanced National Seismic System and all data collected is shared in near real time with them.

  • Center for Earthquake Research and Information

    CERI at the University of Memphis is our primary regional partner. We submit continuous data to them that is used to detect and locate earthquakes within the southeastern US. They are considered the authoritative regional network and are responsible for submitting earthquake locations to the USGS.

  • EarthScope Consortium

    EarthScope supports research in the geosciences and is the primary data archive for seismic data, supporting seismology research in the US. The SCSN shares all data collected in near real time with EarthScope.

  • South Carolina Emergency Management Division

    SCEMD is the state agency within South Carolina responsible for emergency management and preparation.
